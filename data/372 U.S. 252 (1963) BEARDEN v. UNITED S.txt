U.S. Supreme Court  BEARDEN v. UNITED STATES, 372 U.S. 252 (1963) 372 U.S. 252 BEARDEN v. UNITED STATES.  ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE UNITED STATES COURT OF APPEALS  FOR THE FIFTH CIRCUIT. No. 467, Misc. Decided February 25, 1963.  Certiorari granted; judgment vacated; and case remanded. Reported below: 304 F.2d 532. William C. Collins for petitioner. Solicitor General Cox, Assistant Attorney General Miller and Philip R. Monahan for the United States.  PER CURIAM.  The motion for leave to proceed in forma pauperis and the petition for writ of certiorari are granted. The judgment is vacated and the case is remanded for further consideration in light of Elchuk v. United States, 370 U.S. 722. MR. JUSTICE WHITE took no part in the consideration or decision of this case.
