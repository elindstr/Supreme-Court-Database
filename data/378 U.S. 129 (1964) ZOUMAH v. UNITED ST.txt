U.S. Supreme Court  ZOUMAH v. UNITED STATES, 378 U.S. 129 (1964) 378 U.S. 129 ZOUMAH v. UNITED STATES.  ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE UNITED STATES COURT OF APPEALS  FOR THE SEVENTH CIRCUIT. No. 1130, Misc. Decided June 15, 1964.  Certiorari granted; judgment vacated; and case remanded. Petitioner pro se. Solicitor General Cox, Assistant Attorney General Miller, Beatrice Rosenberg and Theodore George Gilinsky for the United States.  PER CURIAM.  The motion for leave to proceed in forma pauperis and the petition for a writ of certiorari are granted. The judgment is vacated and the case is remanded to the Court of Appeals in light of the recommendation of the Solicitor General and upon examination of all the papers filed in the case.
