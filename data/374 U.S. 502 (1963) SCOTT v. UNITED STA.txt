U.S. Supreme Court  SCOTT v. UNITED STATES, 374 U.S. 502 (1963) 374 U.S. 502 SCOTT v. UNITED STATES. ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE UNITED STATES COURT OF APPEALS FOR  THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA CIRCUIT. No. 694, Misc. Decided June 17, 1963.  Certiorari granted; judgment vacated; and case remanded. Petitioner pro se. Solicitor General Cox, Assistant Attorney General Miller, Beatrice Rosenberg and Julia P. Cooper for the United States.  PER CURIAM.  The motion for leave to proceed in forma pauperis and the petition for writ of certiorari are granted. In light of the suggestion of the Solicitor General the judgment is vacated and the case is remanded for reconsideration of the request for a transcript of record.
