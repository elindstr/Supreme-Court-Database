U.S. Supreme Court  BENTLEY v. ALASKA, 374 U.S. 107 (1963) 374 U.S. 107 BENTLEY v. ALASKA.  ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE SUPREME COURT OF ALASKA.  No. 648, Misc. Decided June 10, 1963.  Certiorari granted; judgment vacated; and case remanded. Petitioner pro se. George N. Hayes, Attorney General of Alaska, and John K. Brubaker, Assistant Attorney General, for respondent.  PER CURIAM.  The motion for leave to proceed in forma pauperis and the petition for writ of certiorari are granted. The judgment is vacated and the case is remanded to the Supreme Court of Alaska for reconsideration in light of Brady v. Maryland, 373 U.S. 83, and Mooney v. Holohan, 294 U.S. 103.
