U.S. Supreme Court  PERMIAN CO. v. RAILROAD COMM'N., 358 U.S. 37 (1958) 358 U.S. 37 PERMIAN BASIN PIPELINE CO. v. RAILROAD COMMISSION OF TEXAS ET AL.  APPEAL FROM THE COURT OF CIVIL APPEALS OF TEXAS, THIRD SUPREME JUDICIAL  DISTRICT. No. 64. Decided October 13, 1958.  Appeal dismissed and certiorari denied. Reported below: 302 S.W.2d 238. Lawrence I. Shaw, F. Vinson Roach and R. Dean Moorhead for appellant. Will Wilson, Attorney General of Texas, and James N. Ludlum, First Assistant Attorney General, for the Railroad Commission of Texas, and Charles L. Black for the Atlantic Refining Co., appellees.  PER CURIAM.  The motion to dismiss is granted and the appeal is dismissed. Treating the papers whereon the appeal was taken as a petition for writ of certiorari, certiorari is denied. MR. JUSTICE BLACK is of the opinion that probable jurisdiction should be noted.
